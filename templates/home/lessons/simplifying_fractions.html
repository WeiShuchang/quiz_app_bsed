Simplifying Fractions

What is Simplifying Fractions?

Simplifying fractions means reducing a fraction to its simplest form, where the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1. A fraction is in its simplest form when it cannot be reduced any further without changing its value.

Steps to Simplify Fractions Using the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

  • Step 1: Identify the Fraction.
    Start with the fraction you want to simplify, noting the numerator (the top number) and the denominator (the bottom number).

  • Step 2: Find the Greatest Common Factor (GCF).
    The GCF is the largest positive number that divides two or more integers without leaving a remainder. Here’s how you find it:

    • List the Factors
      Determine all factors of both the numerator and denominator.

    • Identify Common Factors:
      Find the factors that are common to both the numerator and denominator.

    • Determine the GCF:
      Select the largest number from the common factors list. This is your GCF.
  • Step 3: Divide by the GCF.
    Divide both the numerator and denominator by the GCF to simplify the fraction.

Example 1:

Simplify the fraction 2436:

  • Step 1: Identify the Fraction: The numerator is 24, and the denominator is 36.

  • Step 2: Find the GCF:
    • Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24
    • Factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36
    • Common Factors: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

    • The GCF is 12.


  • Step 3: Divide by the GCF: Divide both 24 and 36 by 12:

    24 ÷ 1236 ÷ 12 = 23

    The simplified version of the fraction is 23.

Example 2:

Simplify the fraction 5075:

  • Step 1: Identify the Fraction: The numerator is 50, and the denominator is 75.

  • Step 2: Find the GCF:
    • Factors of 50: 1, 2, 5, 10, 25, 50
    • Factors of 75: 1, 3, 5, 15, 25, 75
    • Common Factors: 1, 5, 25

    • The GCF is 25.


  • Step 3: Divide by the GCF: Divide both 50 and 75 by 25:

    50 ÷ 2575 ÷ 25 = 23

    The simplified version of the fraction is 23.

Remember:

Always ensure that the fraction is fully simplified, and there are no common factors between the numerator and the denominator other than 1. This will ensure that the fraction is in its simplest form.
