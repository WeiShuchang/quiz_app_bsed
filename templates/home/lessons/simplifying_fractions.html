Simplifying Fractions

What is Simplifying Fractions?

Simplifying fractions means reducing a fraction to its simplest form, where the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1. A fraction is in its simplest form when it cannot be reduced any further without changing its value.

Steps to Simplify Fractions Using the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

  • Step 1: Identify the Fraction. Start with the fraction you want to simplify, noting the numerator (the top number) and the denominator (the bottom number).
  • Step 2: Find the Greatest Common Factor (GCF). The GCF is the largest positive number that divides two or more integers without leaving a remainder. Here’s how you find it:
    • List the Factors: Determine all factors of both the numerator and denominator.
    • Identify Common Factors: Find the factors that are common to both the numerator and denominator.
    • Determine the GCF: Select the largest number from the common factors list. This is your GCF.
  • Step 3: Divide by the GCF. Divide both the numerator and denominator by the GCF to simplify the fraction.

Example:

Simplify the fraction 3648:

  • Step 1: Identify the Fraction: The numerator is 36, and the denominator is 48.
  • Step 2: Find the GCF:
    • Factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36
    • Factors of 48: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 48
    • Common Factors: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
    • The GCF is 12.
  • Step 3: Divide by the GCF: Divide both 36 and 48 by 12:
    3612 ÷ 4812 = 34

Remember:

Always ensure that the fraction is fully simplified, and there are no common factors between the numerator and the denominator other than 1. This will ensure that the fraction is in its simplest form.
