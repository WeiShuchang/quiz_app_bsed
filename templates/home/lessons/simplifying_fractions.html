Simplifying Fractions

Simplifying fractions means reducing a fraction to its simplest form, where the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1. A fraction is in its simplest form when it cannot be reduced any further without changing its value.

Key Terms

  • Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

    The Greatest Common Factor, also known as the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD), is defined as the largest positive whole number that divides two or more integers without leaving a remainder.

  • Prime Factorization

    Prime factorization is the process of expressing a number as the product of its prime factors. A prime number is a whole number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.

Steps to Find the GCD

  • Step 1: List the Factors.
    Determine all factors of both the numerator and denominator.

  • Step 2: Identify Common Factors.
    Find the factors that are common to both the numerator and denominator.

  • Step 3: Determine the GCF.
    Select the largest number from the common factors list. This is your GCF.

Methods to Simplify Fractions

Method A: Using the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

  • Step 1: Identify the Fraction.
    Start with the fraction you want to simplify, noting the numerator and the denominator.

  • Step 2: Find the Greatest Common Factor.
    Determine the GCF of the numerator and denominator.

  • Step 3: Divide by the GCF.
    Divide both the numerator and denominator by the GCF to simplify the fraction.

Example: Simplify 36 48

  • 1. Identify the Fraction.

    • The numerator is 36 and the denominator is 48.

  • 2. Find the GCF.
    • List the Factors:

      • Factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36

      • Factors of 48: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 48

    • Identify Common Factors:

      • Common factors of 36 and 48: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

    • Determine the GCF:

      • The GCF is 12.

  • 3. Divide by the GCF.

    • 36 ÷ 12 = 3 48 ÷ 12 = 4

    • The simplified fraction is 3 4 .

Methods to Simplify Fractions

Method B: Using Prime Factorization

  • Step 1: Find the Prime Factorization.
    Determine the prime factors of both the numerator and the denominator.

  • Step 2: Write the Fraction with Prime Factors.
    Rewrite the fraction using its prime factorizations.

  • Step 3: Cancel Common Factors.
    Identify and cancel out any common factors in the numerator and denominator.

  • Step 4: Write the Reduced Fraction.
    The remaining factors give you the simplified fraction.

Example: Simplify 27 45

  • 1. Find the Prime Factorization.

    • Numerator: 27 = 3 × 3 × 3

    • Denominator: 45 = 3 × 3 × 5

  • 2. Write the Fraction with Prime Factors.

    • 27 45 = 3 × 3 × 3 3 × 3 × 5

  • 3. Cancel Common Factors.

    • 3 × 3 × 3 3 × 3 × 5

  • 4. Write the Reduced Fraction.

    • The simplified fraction is 3 5 .
