Multiplying Fractions

What is Multiplying Fractions?

Multiplying fractions is a straightforward process that involves multiplying the numerators and denominators separately.

Steps to Multiply Fractions

  • Step 1: Multiply the Numerators.
    Multiply the top numbers (numerators) of the fractions.
  • Step 2: Multiply the Denominators.
    Multiply the bottom numbers (denominators) of the fractions.
  • Step 3: Combine the Results.
    Write the product of the numerators over the product of the denominators.
  • Step 4: Simplify if Necessary.
    If possible, simplify the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

Example:

Multiply the fractions 34 and 12:

  • Step 1: Multiply the Numerators:

    Multiply 3 by 1:
    3 x 1 = 3.

  • Step 2: Multiply the Denominators:

    Multiply 4 by 2:
    4 x 2 = 8.

  • Step 3: Combine the Results:

    The result is 38.

  • Step 4: Simplify if Necessary:

    The fraction 38 is already in its simplest form.

Remember:

Multiplying fractions is straightforward, but always check if you can simplify your result to its lowest terms.
