Adding and Subtracting Fractions

What is Adding and Subtracting Fractions?

Adding fractions involves combining two or more fractions to produce a single fraction. Subtracting fractions involves finding the difference between two fractions. The process can vary depending on whether the fractions have the same denominator (like terms) or different denominators (unlike terms).

Case 1: Adding Fractions with the Same Denominator

  • Step 1: Add the Numerators. Simply combine the numerators.
  • Step 2: Keep the Denominator. The denominator remains unchanged.
  • Step 3: Simplify if Necessary. If possible, simplify the resulting fraction.

Example:

What is the sum of 16 + 26?

  • Step 1: Add the Numerators: 1 + 2 = 3
  • Step 2: Keep the Denominator: The denominator remains 6.
  • Step 3: Simplify if Necessary: The sum is 36, which simplifies to 12.

Case 2: Adding Fractions with Different Denominators

  • Step 1: Find the Least Common Denominator (LCD). Determine the smallest number that both denominators can divide into evenly.
  • Step 2: Convert Each Fraction. Adjust each fraction to have the common denominator.
  • Step 3: Add the Numerators. Now that both fractions have a common denominator, add their numerators.
  • Step 4: Keep the Common Denominator. The denominator remains as is.
  • Step 5: Simplify if Necessary. Check if the resulting fraction can be simplified further.

Example:

Add 12 + 13.

  • Step 1: Find the LCD: The LCD of 2 and 3 is 6.
  • Step 2: Convert Each Fraction:
    • Convert 12 to 36 (multiply by 3).
    • Convert 13 to 26 (multiply by 2).
  • Step 3: Add the Numerators: 3 + 2 = 5
  • Step 4: Keep the Common Denominator: The denominator is 6.
  • Step 5: Simplify if Necessary: The sum is 56, which is already in its simplest form.

Subtracting Fractions

Subtracting fractions involves finding the difference between two fractions. The process varies depending on whether the fractions have the same or different denominators.

Case 1: Subtracting Fractions with the Same Denominator

  • Step 1: Subtract the Numerators. Directly subtract the numerator of the second fraction from the first fraction.
  • Step 2: Keep the Denominator. The denominator remains unchanged.
  • Step 3: Simplify if Necessary. If possible, reduce the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

Example:

What is 925 - 625?

  • Step 1: Subtract the Numerators: 9 - 6 = 3
  • Step 2: Keep the Denominator: The denominator remains 25.
  • Step 3: Simplify if Necessary: The result is 325, which is already in simplest form.

Case 2: Subtracting Fractions with Different Denominators

  • Step 1: Find the Least Common Denominator (LCD). Determine the smallest number that both denominators can divide into evenly.
  • Step 2: Convert Each Fraction. Adjust each fraction to have the common denominator.
  • Step 3: Subtract the Numerators. Subtract the numerator of the second fraction from the first.
  • Step 4: Keep the Common Denominator. The denominator remains as is.
  • Step 5: Simplify if Necessary. Check if the resulting fraction can be simplified further.

Example:

Subtract 34 - 12.

  • Step 1: Find the LCD: The LCD of 4 and 2 is 4.
  • Step 2: Convert Each Fraction:
    • The first fraction remains 34.
    • Convert 12 to 24 (multiply by 2).
  • Step 3: Subtract the Numerators: 3 - 2 = 1
  • Step 4: Keep the Common Denominator: The denominator is 4.
  • Step 5: Simplify if Necessary: The result is 14, which is already in its simplest form.

Notes:

When adding or subtracting fractions, it's essential to check if the resulting fraction can be simplified. Practicing with various fractions helps in gaining confidence in these operations.
