Adding and Subtracting Fractions

In adding and subtracting fractions, the process can vary depending on whether the fractions are similar or dissimilar. Similar fractions have the same denominators, while dissimilar fractions have different denominators. To add or subtract dissimilar fractions, you must first find the Least Common Denominator (LCD).

Key Terms

  • Least Common Denominator (LCD)

    The Least Common Denominator is the smallest number that can be used as a common denominator for a set of fractions. It is the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators of those fractions.

  • Minuend

    The minuend is the number from which another number is subtracted. It is the first number in a subtraction operation.

  • Subtrahend

    The subtrahend is the number that is subtracted from the minuend. It is the second number in a subtraction operation.

Steps to Find the LCD

  • Step 1: List the Multiples.
    Write down several multiples of each denominator.

  • Step 2: Identify the Smallest Common Multiple.
    Find the smallest number that appears in both lists of multiples.

Case 1: Adding and Subtracting Similar Fractions

A. Adding dissimilar fractions.

  • Definition:

    Let ac and bc be two similar fractions, then a + bc.


  • Note:

    In adding similar fractions, simply add the numerators and keep the denominator. Then simplify if possible.

Addition Examples

  • 1 6 + 2 6 = 1 + 2 6 = 3 6 = 12


  • ( - 1 8 ) + 5 8 = -1 + 5 8 = 4 8 = 1 2

B. Subtraction

  • Definition:

    Let ac and bc be two similar fractions, then a - bc.


  • Note:

    In subtracting similar fractions, simply subtract the numerators and keep the denominator. Then simplify if possible. Remember, do not interchange the minuend and subtrahend.

Subtraction Examples

  • 9 25 - 6 25 = 3 25

  • 1 7 - (- 5 7 ) = 1-(-5) 7 = 1+5 7 = 6 7

Case 2: Adding Fractions and Subtracting Dissimilar Fractions

A. Adding Dissimilar Fractions

  • Definition:

    Let a b and c d be two dissimilar fractions. Also let, m = LCD [b,d]
    then, a b + c d = a( m b ) + c( m d ) m


Steps in Adding Dissimilar Fractions

  • Step 1: Find the LCD

  • Step 2: Adjust the fractions

    • Rewrite each fraction using the LCD:

      • for a b , it would be rewritten as this a( m b ) m

      • for c d , it would be rewritten as this c( m d ) m


  • Step 3: Add the fractions. Combine the adjusted numerators over the common denominator.

    • a( m b ) + c( m d ) m

Example 1: Add 1 2 and 1 3

  • 1. Find the LCD.

    • Multiples of 2: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10

    • Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15

    • The lowest common multiple is 6. Therefore, the LCD is 6.

  • 2. Adjust the fractions.

    • 1( 6 3 ) 6 = 1 (2) 6 = 3 6

    • 1( 6 2 ) 6 = 1 (3) 6 = 2 6

  • 3. Add the fractions.

    • 3 6 + 2 6 = 5 6

Example 2: Add 2 3 and - ( 4 5 )

  • 1. Find the LCD.

    • Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15

    • Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25

    • The lowest common multiple is 15. Therefore, the LCD is 15.

  • 2. Adjust the fractions.

    • 2( 15 3 ) 15 = 1 (5) 15 = 5 15

    • -4( 15 5 ) 15 = -4 (3) 15 = -12 15

  • 3. Add the fractions.

    • 5 15 + (-12) 15 = -7 15

B. Subtracting dissimilar fractions

  • Definition:

    Let a b and c d be two dissimilar fractions. Also let, m = LCD [b,d]
    then, a b - c d = a( m b ) - c( m d ) m


Steps in Subtracting Dissimilar Fractions

  • Step 1: Find the LCD

  • Step 2: Adjust the fractions

    • Rewrite each fraction using the LCD:

      • for a b , it would be rewritten as this a( m b ) m

      • for c d , it would be rewritten as this c( m d ) m


  • Step 3: Subtract the fractions. Subtract the adjusted numerators over the common denominator.

    • a( m b ) - c( m d ) m

What is 3 4 - 1 2 ?

  • 1. Find the LCD.

    • Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15

    • Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20

    • The lowest common multiple is 12. Therefore, the LCD is 12.

  • 2. Adjust the fractions.

    • 3( 12 4 ) 12 = 3 (3) 12 = 9 12

    • 1( 12 2 ) 12 = 1 (6) 12 = 6 12

  • 3. Subtract the fractions.

    • 9 12 - 6 12 = 3 12 = 1 4

What is -( 3 5 ) - 1 3 ?

  • 1. Find the LCD.

    • Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25

    • Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15

    • The lowest common multiple is 15. Therefore, the LCD is 15.

  • 2. Adjust the fractions.

    • -3( 15 5 ) 15 = -3 (3) 15 = 9 15

    • 1( 15 3 ) 15 = (5) 15 = 5 15

  • 3. Subtract the fractions.

    • -9 15 - 5 15 = -14 15 = - 14 15

ADDITIONAL CASES

  • A. Adding or Subtracting a Whole Number and a Fraction

    Adding and subtracting a whole number and a fraction involves converting the whole number into fraction form then proceeding with usual addition or subtraction of fractions.


  • Steps:

    • 1. Convert the whole number into a fraction by placing it over 1.

    • 2. Perform the usual addition or subtraction operation.

Example 1: What is 3 + 5 6 ?

  • 1. Convert the whole number into a fraction by placing it over 1.

    • 3 1 + 5 6

  • 2.Perform addition operation.

    • a. Find the LCD.

      • The lowest common multiple of 1 and 6 is 6. Therefore, the LCD is 6.

    • b. Adjust the fractions.

      • 3( 6 1 ) 6 = 3(6) 6 = 18 6

      • 5( 6 6 ) 6 = 5(1) 6 = 5 6

    • c. Add the Fractions

      • 18 6 + 5 6 = 23 6 or can be expressed into improper fraction as 3 5 6 .

Example2: What is 3 5 - 5 ?

  • 1. Convert the whole number into a fraction by placing it over 1.

    • 3 5 + 5 1

  • 2.Perform addition operation.

    • a. Find the LCD.

      • The lowest common multiple of 5 and 1 is 2. Therefore, the LCD is 5.

    • b. Adjust the fractions.

      • 3( 5 5 ) 5 = 3(1) 5 = 3 5

      • 5( 5 1 ) 5 = 5(5) 5 = 21 5

    • c. Subtract Fractions

      • 3 5 - 25 5 = -22 5 or can be expressed into improper fraction as -4 2 5 .

  • B.Adding and Subtracting a Mixed Number to a Fraction or another Mixed Number

    Adding and subtracting a mixed number to a fraction or another mixed number involves converting the mixed number into fraction form then proceeding with usual addition or subtraction of fractions.


  • Steps:

    • 1. Convert the mixed number into an improper fraction.

    • 2. Perform the usual addition or subtraction operation.

Example 1: 3 1 4 + 5 6

  • 1. Convert the mixed number into an improper fraction.

    • 13 4 + 5 6

  • 2.Perform addition operation.

    • a. Find the LCD.

      • Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20

      • Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 45, 30

      • The lowest common multiple is 12. Therefore, the LCD is 12.

    • b. Adjust the fractions.

      • 13( 12 4 ) 12 = 13(3) 12 = 39 12

      • 5( 12 6 ) 12 = 5(2) 12 = 10 12

    • c. Add the Fractions

      • 39 12 + 10 12 = 49 12 or can be expressed into improper fraction as 4 1 12 .

Example 2: 3 1 4 - 5 2 3

  • 1. Convert the whole number into an improper fraction.

    • 13 4 - 17 3

  • 2. Perform subtraction operation.

    • a. Find the LCD.

      • Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20

      • Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15

      • The lowest common multiple of12. Therefore, the LCD is 12.

    • b. Adjust the fractions.

      • 13( 12 4 ) 12 = 13(3) 12 = 39 12

      • 17( 12 3 ) 12 = 17(4) 12 = 68 12

    • c. Subtract the Fractions

      • 39 12 + 68 12 = -29 12 or can be expressed into improper fraction as -2 5 12 .

Alternative Method: Butterfly Method

  • The Butterfly Method is a visual and systematic approach to adding and subtracting fractions, designed to simplify the process without requiring a common denominator.


  • Steps of the Butterfly Method

    • 1. Draw the Butterfly: Write the two fractions side by side and draw "wings" diagonally across the fractions, connecting the numerator of one fraction to the denominator of the other. This creates a visual representation resembling a butterfly.

    • 2. Diagonal Multiplication:

      • Multiply the numerator of the first fraction by the denominator of the second fraction.

      • Multiply the numerator of the second fraction by the denominator of the first fraction.

    • 3. Calculate New Numerator:

      • For addition:
        Add the two products obtained from the diagonal multiplications to get the new numerator.

      • For subtraction:
        Subtract the two products obtained from the diagonal multiplications to get the new numerator. Remember, subtract the second product from the first product.

    • 4. Find New Denominator:
      Multiply both denominators together to find the new denominator.

    • 5. Formulate Final Fraction and Simplify if Needed:
      The final result is expressed as a fraction with the new numerator over the new denominator. Then simplify if needed.
