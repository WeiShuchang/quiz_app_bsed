Expressing Improper Fractions to Mixed Numbers and Vice Versa

Understanding how to convert between improper fractions and mixed numbers is essential for mastering fraction operations. Let's review the key terms and their definitions.

Key Terms:

  • Improper Fraction

    A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.
    ( a b , where a ≥ b )

  • Mixed Number

    A number that combines an integer with a proper fraction.
    ( a b c , where a is the whole number and b c is the proper fraction. )

A. Expressing Improper Fractions to Mixed Numbers

If a b is an improper fraction, then the mixed number equivalent is a c d , where a is the whole number, c is the remainder, and d is the original denominator.

Steps to Express:

  • 1. Divide the Numerator by the Denominator.

    • The quotient becomes the whole number part of the mixed number.

    • The remainder becomes the new numerator, while the denominator remains the same.

  • 2. Combine to Create the Mixed Number.

Example 1: Express 9 4 to a mixed number.

  • 1. Divide the Numerator by the Denominator.

    Divide 9 by 4: The quotient is 2 and the remainder is 1.

  • 2. Combine to Create the Mixed Number.

    The mixed number is 2 1 4 .

Example 2: Express - 11 3 to a mixed number.

  • 1. Divide the Numerator by the Denominator.

    Divide -11 by 3: The quotient is -3 and the remainder is 2.
    Whole number: -3
    Proper fraction: 2 3

  • 2. Combine to Create the Mixed Number.

    The mixed number is -3 2 3 .

B. Expressing Mixed Numbers to Improper Fractions

If a b c is a mixed number, then the improper fraction equal to this mixed number is ac + b c .

Steps to Express:

  • 1. Multiply the Whole Number by the Denominator.
  • 2. Add the Numerator.
  • 3. Write as an Improper Fraction.

    • The original denominator will be the denominator of the improper fraction.

Example 1: Express 2 1 8 to a mixed number.

  • 1. Multiply the Whole Number to the Denominator.

    • 8 (2)= 16

  • 2. Add the Numerator.

    • 16 + 1= 17

  • 3. Write as an improper fraction.

    • 17 8

Example 2: Express 3 2 5 to an improper fraction.

  • 1. Multiply the Whole Number to the Denominator.

    • -3 (5) = -15

  • 2. Add the Numerator.

    • -15 + 2 = -13

  • 3. Write as an improper fraction.

    • -13 5 or - 13 5
