Converting Mixed Fractions and Improper Fractions

What are Mixed Numbers and Improper Fractions?

An improper fraction has a numerator that is larger than or equal to its denominator (e.g., 9/4), while a mixed number combines a whole number with a proper fraction (e.g., 2 1/4).

Converting Improper Fractions to Mixed Numbers

  • Step 1: Divide the Numerator by the Denominator.
    In the example 94, divide 9 by 4.
    Explanation: You are seeing how many whole 4's can fit into 9. The answer is 2 (since 4 × 2 = 8), with a remainder of 1.
  • Step 2: Write the Whole Number.
    The quotient (the result of division) is the whole number part, which is 2 in this case.
  • Step 3: Create the Proper Fraction.
    The remainder becomes the new numerator, and the denominator stays the same. In this case, the remainder is 1, so the fraction becomes 14.
  • Step 4: Combine.
    Put the whole number and the proper fraction together. The mixed number is 2 14.

Example:

Convert 94 to a mixed number:

  • Step 1: Divide: 9 ÷ 4 = 2 remainder 1.

  • Step 2: Whole number: 2.

  • Step 3: Proper fraction: 14.

  • Step 4: Mixed number: 2 14.

Converting Mixed Numbers to Improper Fractions

  • Step 1: Multiply the Whole Number by the Denominator.
    In the mixed number 2 14, multiply the whole number 2 by the denominator 4.
    Explanation: 2 × 4 = 8, so you have 8 parts from the whole number.
  • Step 2: Add the Numerator.
    Now, add the numerator (1) to the result from Step 1. This gives you 8 + 1 = 9.
  • Step 3: Write as an Improper Fraction.
    The sum (9) becomes the numerator, and the denominator remains the same (4). The improper fraction is 94.

Example:

Convert 214 to an improper fraction:

  • Step 1: Multiply: 2 × 4 = 8.

  • Step 2: Add: 8 + 1 = 9.

  • Step 3: Improper fraction: 94.

Remember to practice converting between mixed numbers and improper fractions. Understanding these conversions will help with more complex fraction operations. In our next lesson, we’ll explore adding and subtracting fractions!
