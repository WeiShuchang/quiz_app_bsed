Understanding Fractions

Definition

A fraction is a numerical representation of a part of a whole. It consists of two numbers separated by a horizontal line called the fraction bar:

  • Numerator:
    The number above the fraction bar, indicating how many parts are being considered.
  • Denominator:
    The number below the fraction bar, indicating the total number of equal parts into which the whole is divided.

Examples:

In the fraction 34,

  • The numerator 3 indicates that 3 parts are being considered.

  • The denominator 4 indicates that the whole is divided into 4 equal parts.

In the fraction 67,

  • The numerator 6 indicates that 6 parts are being considered.

  • The denominator 7 indicates that the whole is divided into 7 equal parts.

Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fractions:

    The numerator is less than the denominator.

    Examples:

    25 1117 313

  • Improper Fractions:

    The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

    Examples:

    55 97 258

  • Mixed Numbers:

    A whole number combined with a proper fraction.

    Examples:

    1 12 12 37 20 15
