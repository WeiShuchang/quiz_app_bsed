Understanding Fractions

Definition

A fraction is a numerical representation of a part of a whole. It consists of two numbers separated by a horizontal line called the fraction bar:

a b

  • Numerator(a):
    The number above the fraction bar, indicating how many parts are being considered.
  • Denominator(b):
    The number below the fraction bar, indicating the total number of equal parts into which the whole is divided.

Examples:

In the fraction 34,

  • The numerator 3 indicates that 3 parts are being considered.

  • The denominator 4 indicates that the whole is divided into 4 equal parts.

In the fraction 78,

  • The numerator 7 indicates that 7 parts are being considered.

  • The denominator 8 indicates that the whole is divided into 8 equal parts.

Types of Fractions

  • A. According to the Relationship Between the Numerator and Denominator
    • Proper Fractions:

      The numerator is less than the denominator.

      Examples:

      25, 1117, - 313

    • Improper Fractions:

      The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

      Examples:

      32, 97, - 258

  • B.According to the Relationship of their Denominators
    • Similar Fractions:

      These are fractions that have the same denominator. They are also known as like fractions.

      Examples:

      17, 37, 57, 67

    • Dissimilar Fractions:

      These fractions have different denominators. They are also referred to as unlike fractions.

      Examples:

      13, 37, 49, 711
