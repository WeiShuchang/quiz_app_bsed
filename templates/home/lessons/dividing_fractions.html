Dividing Fractions

Key Terms:

  • Dividend

    The dividend is the first number in a division operation; it is the number that you want to divide.

  • Divisor:

    The divisor is the second number in a division operation; it is the number by which you divide the dividend.

  • Reciprocal:

    The reciprocal of a fraction is obtained by flipping the fraction, which means swapping its numerator and denominator. For a fraction a b where a is the numerator and b is the denominator, the reciprocal is b a

  • Definition

    Given the fractions, a b and c d , b and d ≠ 0, then a b ÷ c d = a b × d c = ad bc

Steps

  • 1. Take the reciprocal of the divisor.
  • 2. Perform usual multiplication.

  • Note: In terms wherein a whole number is divided by a fraction or a mixed number is divided by a fraction or another mixed number, convert these numbers first into fraction form then proceed with continue with division.

  • 2 3 ÷ 3 4 = 2 3 × 4 3 = 2(4) 3(3) = 8 9

Example 4:

Alternate Methods for Dividing Fractions:

  • A. Cross Multiplication

    Example: 2 5 ÷ 5 3

  • 1. Multiply the first cross term

    Multiply the numerator of the first fraction to the denominator of the second fraction making it the new numerator.

    • 2 × 3 = 6

  • 2. Multiply the second cross term:

    Multiply the numerator of the second fraction to the denominator of the first fraction making it the new denominator.

    • 5 × 5 = 25

  • 3. Set-up the new fraction.

    Multiply the numerator of the second fraction to the denominator of the first fraction making it the new denominator.

    • 6 25

  • 4. Simplify if necessary:

    If possible, reduce the resulting fraction into its simplest form.

    • 6 25 is already in it's simpliest form.
