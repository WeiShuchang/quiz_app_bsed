Dividing Fractions

What is Dividing Fractions?

Dividing fractions involves multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction. This process makes it easier to compute the result.

Steps for Dividing Fractions

  • Step 1: Find the Reciprocal
    Flip the second fraction to get its reciprocal.
  • Step 2: Multiply the Fractions
    Multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction.
  • Step 3: Simplify if Necessary
    If possible, reduce the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

Example:

What is 23 ÷ 45?

  • Step 1: Find the Reciprocal: The reciprocal of 45 is 54.

  • Step 2: Multiply the Fractions: 23 × 54 = 1012.

  • Step 3: Simplify if Necessary: The result is 1012, which simplifies to 56.

Another Example:

What is 34 ÷ 23?

  • Step 1: Find the Reciprocal: The reciprocal of 23 is 32.

  • Step 2: Multiply the Fractions: 34 × 32 = 98.

  • Step 3: Simplify if Necessary: The result is 98, which is already in its simplest form.
