Dividing Fractions

What is Dividing Fractions?

Dividing fractions involves a specific process that requires converting the division operation into multiplication.

Steps to Divide Fractions

  • Step 1: Take the Reciprocal. Take the reciprocal of the second fraction by flipping its numerator and denominator.
  • Step 2: Multiply. Change the division operation to multiplication by multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction.
  • Step 3: Multiply the Numerators. Multiply the numerators of both fractions together.
  • Step 4: Multiply the Denominators. Multiply the denominators of both fractions together.
  • Step 5: Combine the Results. Form a new fraction using the results.
  • Step 6: Simplify if Necessary. If possible, reduce the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

Example:

Divide the fractions 23 by 34:

  • Step 1: Take the Reciprocal:
    The reciprocal of 34 is 43.
  • Step 2: Multiply:
    23 x 43
  • Step 3: Multiply the Numerators:
    2 x 4 = 8
  • Step 4: Multiply the Denominators:
    3 x 3 = 9
  • Step 5: Combine the Results:
    The result is 89.
  • Step 6: Simplify if Necessary:
    The fraction 89 is already in its simplest form.

Remember:

When dividing fractions, always take the reciprocal of the second fraction before multiplying!
